Compact Adjustable Laser Module

Precision focusing up to 15 meters

CALM transforms fixed-focus laser modules into adaptable optical systems. Adjustable Galilean lens spacing maintains beam concentration across variable distances — without increasing laser power.

The challenge

Fixed-focus lasers lose energy at variable distances

Compact laser modules are typically optimized for a single focal distance. As the target plane moves closer or farther away, beam divergence increases spot size and spreads optical power over a larger area — reducing irradiance and weakening performance.

Simply increasing laser power adds thermal load, cost, and safety requirements. The smarter engineering path is to improve how the existing beam is delivered to the target.

What happens without adaptive focusing

  • Beam spreads as distance changes
  • Energy density drops at the target plane
  • Fixed geometry limits operational flexibility
  • Higher power becomes the default workaround

The CALM approach

Adjustable optics, not higher power

CALM uses a Galilean lens arrangement — a concave and convex lens pair — inside a compact barrel. A single-axis mechanical adjustment changes lens spacing to shift the focal condition across a 0–15 m operating range.

01

Galilean configuration

Negative and positive lenses modify beam propagation and enable focal shift without changing the laser source.

02

Mechanical adjustability

Single-axis lens spacing control adapts the module to variable target distances with repeatable positioning.

03

Simulation-validated

MATLAB-based 2D ray-tracing confirms the optical layout supports precise focusing up to 15 m.

04

Compact integration

Optical elements are packaged in a cylindrical barrel that maintains axial alignment and compact dimensions.

Applications

Where adaptive focusing matters

CALM is designed for scenarios where target distance changes but beam concentration must remain useful — laboratory optical setups, engineering demonstrations, and compact systems that cannot rely on fixed focal geometry.

  • Variable-distance laser targeting experiments
  • Compact optical prototyping and education
  • Energy-efficient beam delivery research
  • Galilean optics integration studies

Safety notice

CALM is an optical focusing concept for controlled laboratory environments. Any practical laser work requires beam enclosures, certified eye protection, access control, warning labels, and compliance with applicable laser safety standards. Never direct laser output toward people, animals, vehicles, aircraft, or uncontrolled environments.
